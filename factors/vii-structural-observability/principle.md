# Factor VII: Structural Observability

*Every agent action is reconstructable by architecture, not investigation*

When a regulator asks "why was this trade executed," when an incident responder asks "what did the agent do in the eight days before it deleted the database," when a compliance auditor asks "which clinician's authorization was used for this prescription change" — the answer must already exist. Not in application logs that a developer chose to capture. Not in a nightly batch job that parses fragments into a timeline. In a structured record that was produced at the moment of invocation, by the same control plane that performed the operation, as a byproduct of correct architecture.

Every framework in the agentic ecosystem provides some form of tracing. None produces a complete enterprise audit record. They capture what developers need — token counts, latency, error messages — and omit what enterprises require: the authenticated identity of the caller, the intent that entered the control plane, every backend system the operation touched, the domain-level outcome including partial completions and compensations, and a chain of integrity that proves the record was not altered after the fact. A structured trace of an LLM's reasoning is a developer tool. A structured record of the control plane's actions is an architectural commitment.

**When the control plane already enforces deterministic mutations (II), sequences operations with idempotency tokens (V), and returns structured error contracts (VI), the audit trail is not a separate concern — it is the emergent property of work the architecture already does.** One proven pattern: each capability invoked produces a structured record carrying the caller's identity, the declared intent and its contextual identifiers, the version of the skill or orchestrator invoked, every backend system affected with its operation-specific outcome — including partial success where a multi-system operation completed in some backends and triggered compensation in others — and a hash linking this record to its predecessor. The record is append-only, written at invocation time, and immutable once written. The control plane does not produce this record because an auditor asked for it. It produces the record because that is what structured execution looks like.

Regulations are converging on this as a minimum, not a ceiling. PCI-DSS 10.2 requires automatic audit trails with six mandatory fields per event. HIPAA 164.312(b) mandates mechanisms that record and examine activity in systems containing protected health information. The EU AI Act Article 12 requires high-risk AI systems to "technically allow for the automatic recording of events" — an architectural requirement, not a process requirement, enforceable August 2026. SOX Section 802 mandates seven-year retention with criminal penalties for alteration. FINRA's 2026 oversight report specifically warns that AI agent actions may be difficult to reconstruct — and classifies agent outputs as books and records subject to WORM storage. These frameworks assume the record exists at transaction time. When it doesn't — when audit depends on reconstruction from whatever the developer happened to log — the control plane has failed its most basic accountability obligation.

When it is absent, incident response becomes archaeology. The agent did its job. Nobody can prove it. When a true control plane is present, every capability invoked answers five questions before anyone asks them: who requested this, what was the declared intent, which systems were affected, what was the outcome — including partial completions and compensations — and can the record prove it has not been altered. Agent failures become attributable, bounded, and reconstructable by the architecture itself — versus a team leading an incident post-mortem.
